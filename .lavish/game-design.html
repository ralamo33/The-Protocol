A terminal-style text game where you command a secret paranormal research organization. Each anomalous entity that arrives demands your decisions: which experiments to run, which scientist leads them. Your choices ripple outward — unlocking forbidden technologies, triggering cascading consequences, and steering humanity toward one of many fates. Written in clinical SCP-wiki register: detached, procedural, deeply unsettling.

Text + ASCII Art Resource Management Branching Narrative Multiple Endings

◈ CORE GAME LOOP

PHASE 1

INTAKE

A new anomalous entity arrives. Its classification, threat tier, and known properties are displayed. ASCII art renders the entity.

→
PHASE 2

ASSIGNMENT

You select 1–3 experiments to run on the entity, then assign a lead scientist. Each choice costs resources and carries risk.

→
PHASE 3

CONSULTATION

Scientists report back mid-experiment with dilemmas. 2–3 critical decisions per entity that alter outcomes, morale, and containment.

→
PHASE 4

RESOLUTION

Experiment results arrive: breakthroughs, casualties, containment status, and unlocked tech. The next entity is queued.

→
ENDGAME

DIVERGENCE

After all entities are processed, accumulated decisions converge into one of several branching endings.

◈ EXPERIMENT TYPES

STUDY Low Risk

Passive observation and documentation. Yields Knowledge Points, low breakthrough chance, minimal casualties. Required before other experiment types.

INTERACTION Moderate Risk

Direct researcher contact with the entity. High data yield, significant morale/injury risk. Can unlock hidden entity behaviors.

WEAPONIZATION High Risk

Attempts to harness anomalous properties for APEX defense or offense. Fast tech gain, major ethics cost, containment destabilization risk.

CROSS-TEST Variable Risk

Expose one entity to another. Wildcard outcomes — can unlock unique tech not available through any other path, or cause containment cascades.

CONTAINMENT ANALYSIS Low Risk

Focus on stability and long-term housing. Reduces escape risk, generates containment protocols. Low tech gain but protects other experiments.

COMMUNICATION Cognitohazard Risk

Attempt dialogue with the entity. Can unlock entity-specific narrative paths and unique endings. Risk: cognitohazard exposure, researcher mental health events.

◈ ENDINGS

💀 MASS BREACH

BAD

Containment collapses. Multiple entities escape simultaneously. APEX is overrun. No survivors.

Trigger: Containment → 0

📰 EXPOSED

BAD

Trigger: Oversight → max + whistleblower event

🛡 FORTRESS

GOOD

All entities contained. APEX operates in perpetual secrecy. Humanity is safe — but at what cost? A quiet, grey victory.

Trigger: Containment branch maxed + all entities secured

AMBIGUOUS

Trigger: Science branch maxed + Chorus communication

DARK

HIDDEN

Trigger: Communication × 3 + high morale + Dr. Nath survives

✓
PLAYTHROUGH LENGTH
4–5 entities per run (~20 min)

Short, replayable. Each run reveals a different slice of the entity roster and pushes toward different endings.

✓
SCIENTIST LOSS
Permanent death / defection

High tension. Losing a scientist can permanently lock an ending. Assignment decisions carry real weight.

✓
RESOURCE DISPLAY
Vague prose — no numbers

Resources surface as diegetic memos and status reports. Keeps the immersion intact and avoids management-sim feel.

✓
TONE
Clinical dread — SCP-wiki register

Detached, procedural language. Horror comes from what's implied, not stated. Entity descriptions read like real containment files.
