P-01
ARCHIVIST
✓

Obsessive record-keeper. Believes information is the only reliable weapon. Catalogs everything — anomaly behavior, personnel performance, Director decisions. Has a memory that borders on institutional.

WEAKNESS: Paralysis by analysis. Cannot act without complete data. Delays cost cycles.
P-02
SHEPHERD
✓

Charismatic people-keeper. Builds loyalty through catastrophe. Personnel morale improves measurably under their influence. Knows every researcher's name and what they fear.

WEAKNESS: Protects personnel over mission. Flinches at necessary sacrifice. Morale-first, results-second.
P-03
CARTOGRAPHER
✓

Sees structure in chaos. Brilliant lateral thinker who maps containment problems no one else thought to draw. First to propose novel protocols.

WEAKNESS: Pattern recognition bleeds into paranoia. Finds threats that don't exist. Hard to distinguish insight from noise.
P-04
VIGIL
✓

Ice-cold tactician. Makes hard calls without pause, without explanation, without regret. Results-driven to a degree that other Principals find disturbing.

WEAKNESS: Treats personnel as expendable. Callousness causes silent attrition — people leave, quit, break without warning.
P-05
TESTAMENT
✓

Former ethicist. Chose containment as the lesser evil. Keeps the Foundation from forgetting what it costs to be human. Pushes back on Director decisions that cross the line.

WEAKNESS: Moral hesitation at critical moments. Stalls decisions requiring urgency. The line they won't cross may be exactly the line you need to cross.
P-06
HOLLOW
✓

Anomaly survivor. Unique behavioral insight into containment subjects that no trained researcher can replicate. Something was changed by what they went through. They don't deny it.

WEAKNESS: Psychological instability. Unreliable under sustained pressure. Exposure may still be ongoing.
P-07
MERIDIAN
✓

Logistics engine. Keeps funding flowing, supply chains intact, personnel rosters full. Without Meridian, operations run dry. With them, you rarely worry about budget.

WEAKNESS: Severe risk aversion. Blocks experimental protocols. Won't approve anything with uncertain return. Budget safety over results.
P-08
CASKET
✓

Security lead. Containment enforcement specialist. Executes breach response, threat neutralization, and personnel control with the minimum of ceremony.

WEAKNESS: Escalation bias. Force is the first and only option. Situations that require negotiation or restraint tend to get worse.
P-09
ORACLE
✓

Predictive analyst. Runs probability models on anomaly behavior, personnel performance, and Director decision trees. Has been right when it mattered most.

WEAKNESS: Communicates in likelihoods, not decisions. Refuses to make calls without sufficient confidence intervals. Analysis without action.
P-10
SIGNAL
✓

Narrative control. Manages communications, public-facing interfaces, and interagency reporting. Makes the Foundation look like exactly what it needs to look like, when it needs to.

WEAKNESS: Prioritizes the story over the truth. Will misrepresent outcomes that should be accurately reported. The cover can become the crisis.

active containment subjects — select 3
Anomalies define the threats this cycle. Their nature shapes every decision you make.
SELECTED: 0 / 3
A-01
WARDEN
✓

Manifests as a senior Foundation official. Indistinguishable from legitimate authority. Psychically rewrites the organizational memories of personnel who report to it. The one already inside.

WEAKNESS: Requires proximity to active Foundation hierarchy. Loses influence when isolated from institutional structure.
A-02
ECHO CHAMBER
✓

A room. Replays past events from within the facility — but altered. Each replay shifts what personnel believe happened. Corrupts institutional memory over time. You can't destroy a thing that lives in minds.

WEAKNESS: Stationary. Influence is proximity-dependent. Personnel who have never entered are unaffected.
A-03
PALE CARTOGRAPHY
✓

A map that is always accurate to future events. Possessing it provides perfect foresight. Studying it long enough removes the will to act — if you know what happens, you stop believing choices matter.

WEAKNESS: Requires sustained study to exert influence. Brief exposure is non-hazardous. Controlled access limits exposure window.
A-04
THE INTERVIEW
✓

An entity that presents as a necessary conversation. Every subject who encounters it believes they must speak to it — urgently, privately, now. The conversation never concludes. Subjects do not return.

WEAKNESS: Cannot initiate contact. Must be sought. Removing the perception of necessity breaks the compulsion cycle.
A-05
SEVERANCE
✓

Memetic pathogen. Spreads through shared documents, briefings, and interagency communications. Infected subjects experience progressive erosion of institutional loyalty. Begin acting in self-interest only.

WEAKNESS: Slow progression. Early infection is reversible. Detection windows exist before behavioral shift is complete.
A-06
UNCONFIRMED
✓

An anomaly no researcher can agree on. Every report describes something different. Containment logs are internally inconsistent. There is no consensus on what it is, what it wants, or if it is one thing at all.

WEAKNESS: Cannot be targeted directly. Containment protocols built around any single theory will fail. Inconsistency is the only consistent fact.
A-07
LITURGY
✓

A ritual text that compels reading and belief. Readers experience it as meaningful, authoritative, and personally relevant. Compliance follows. The purpose served is not the Foundation's.

WEAKNESS: Physical medium dependency. Destruction of source copies disrupts spread. Non-literate personnel are unaffected.
A-08
HOLLOW KING
✓

Humanoid entity with no fixed appearance. Mirrors the authority figures that personnel trust most. Over time, institutional perception shifts — personnel stop recognizing the original and respond only to the copy.

WEAKNESS: Requires a target to mirror. Rotating authority structures disrupts the mirroring cycle. Confusion is its enemy.
A-09
THE LAST RECORD
✓

A file documenting the internal collapse of a previous Foundation iteration. Reading it is believed to create the conditions it describes. Every Foundation that has studied it has followed the same sequence of failures.

WEAKNESS: Passive. Requires voluntary engagement. Personnel who know not to read it are unaffected. The challenge is that it always seems important to read.
A-10
NIGHTWATCH
✓

Passive surveillance entity. Does nothing. Observes everything. It has been watching for [REDACTED] and no action has ever been confirmed. What it is waiting for, and what it will do when it finds it, is unknown.

WEAKNESS: Has never demonstrated active hostility. Containment is precautionary. The risk is entirely in the unknown unknown.
